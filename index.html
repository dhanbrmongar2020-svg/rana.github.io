The Mountaineering Experiment

During my mountaineering adventure, I conducted an experiment to test the effects of altitude on the boiling point of water. I wanted to see how much the lower atmospheric pressure at high elevations would affect the time it took to cook certain foods.

Methodology:

  • Used a portable stove and a thermometer to measure water temperature.
  • Recorded the time to boil at different altitudes (base camp vs. higher elevations).
  • Noted the time required to cook pasta to an al dente state at each location.

Findings:

The experiment confirmed that water boils at a lower temperature at higher altitudes. This resulted in significantly longer cooking times for the pasta, highlighting the practical challenges of cooking and nutrition in high-altitude environments.
